Robotics Manipulation with MPPI

  • An amazing experiment.

    Overview

    MPPI(Model Predictive Path Integral[1])

    The basic idea of this algorithm is derived from the Model Predictive Control, in short, MPC. It first needs us to have the model or at least the approximated dynamic transition function. Then I generate a lot of sampled trajectories through the model. After that, I use a “Free Energy[2]” method to get a “best” action trajectory and move forward with one step.

    • Application of MPPI

    In the paper[1], they tested it on the driving, and here I apply it onto the inverted pendulum and the picking task with a 7-degrees robot arm. I get a very exciting results and the following is the demo video.

    In all these task, I use the simulator as the model and define a simple reward function(Norm2)), without any training or learning. It is totally on-the-fly. To my surprise, there is no problems in the so called “Dimension Curse” from Reinforcement Learning. There is no need for any guidance or imitation. A simple reward and a computer, then all the stuff is done!

    The simulator I use is the MuJoCo platform.
